
RNA SPLICING PROCESS:

DNA (Genomic)
   --> Transcription
pre-mRNA: [Exon1]--intron1--[Exon2]--intron2--[Exon3]
   --> Splicing (introns removed)
mature mRNA: [Exon1][Exon2][Exon3]
   --> Translation
Protein

KEY CONCEPTS:
- Exons: Coding sequences (retained in mature mRNA)
- Introns: Non-coding sequences (removed during splicing)
- Alternative splicing: Different exon combinations -> different proteins
- Splicing errors: Can lead to disease (e.g., incorrect protein function)

DISEASE CONNECTION:
- Aberrant splicing patterns in blood cells can indicate genetic disorders
- Expression levels of spliced transcripts serve as disease biomarkers
- This analysis focuses on mature mRNA expression patterns
